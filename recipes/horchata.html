Agua de Horchata

Description

Agua de horchata is a traditional Mexican beverage made from rice, water, milk, cinnamon, and sugar, often enjoyed for its refreshing and sweet flavor. The drink has a creamy texture and a delightful blend of cinnamon and vanilla notes, making it a popular choice for cooling down on hot days. Sometimes garnished with a sprinkle of ground cinnamon or served over ice, agua de horchata offers a unique and delicious alternative to more common drinks. It is cherished for its simple ingredients and comforting taste, providing a taste of Mexican culture in every sip.

Ingredients

  • Water
  • Rice
  • Sugar
  • Milk
  • Vanilla
  • Cinnamon

Steps

  1. Blend together the water and rice, then let the mixture stand for three hours.
  2. Afterwards, strain the rice into a pitcher, discarding the rice solids.
  3. Add the remaining ingredients to the pitcher and stir well.
  4. Chill the mixture thoroughly and serve it over ice for a refreshing drink.
